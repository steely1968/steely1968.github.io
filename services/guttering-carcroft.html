Frequently Asked Questions

How often should gutters be cleaned?
Most homes need cleaning once or twice a year, usually in spring and autumn. Properties near trees may need it more often because leaves and debris build up faster.

What happens if gutters are blocked?
When they are blocked, water cannot flow away properly. It overflows down the walls and can cause damp, stains, and damage to brickwork and foundations.

What is the difference between cleaning and clearing?
Clearing removes leaves, moss, and debris that cause blockages. Cleaning then washes the system through so water flows freely again.

Can small leaks be fixed?
Yes, most small leaks can be repaired by sealing joints, replacing short sections, or tightening loose parts instead of changing the whole system.

How do I know if repairs are needed?
Look for dripping water, sagging sections, cracks, or water marks on your walls. These are all signs something is not working properly.

When is replacement the better option?
If the system is very old, badly cracked, rusted, or leaking in many places, replacement is usually more practical than constant repairs.

What materials are used today?
Modern systems are made from strong materials like plastic or metal that last longer and cope better with heavy rain.

Do you work on homes and businesses?
Yes, services are available for houses, shops, offices, and larger buildings.

Can damage from bad weather be fixed?
Strong wind and heavy rain can loosen parts or cause leaks. These problems can usually be repaired quickly.

How long does cleaning take?
Most jobs take between one and two hours, depending on the size of the building and how blocked the system is.

Will there be a mess left behind?
No, all waste is removed and the area is left clean and tidy.

Why use a local service?
A nearby team can respond faster, understands local conditions, and provides more reliable support.
